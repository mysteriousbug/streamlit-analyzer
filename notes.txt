2. Risk Descriptions: High number of EID withdrawals has been experienced - H1 ‘2011: 26,000+ changes using EIDs and 157,000+ incidents using EIDs. There is no logging and review of the usage of the EIDs today as Technology lacks the capacity to review each and every withdrawal and usage of these privileged ids.  The reason for the high number of withdrawals of EIDs today is due to the fact that a number of support staff do not have the basic access to allow them to have read access to do investigations nor do they have the access for basic operations.  Therefore, majority of the EIDs withdrawn are for basic BAU work which do not require such high level privileges to do the work.  This leads to the second issue with inappropriate access given for day to day BAU work by IT Support. 
Both inadequacies may result in unauthorized changes taking place undetected which in turn can cause system failures that lead to financial losses, business operational, reputation and regulatory impacts.  There is also a risk of fraud taking place as the EIDs have the ability to patch production data as well as program objects.
